I am a clinical bioinformatician with over five years of experience in NGS data analysis and pipeline troubleshooting in hospital environments, specialized in onco-hematology genomics. I have strong expertise in Linux-based high-performance computing (HPC) environments, command-line workflow design (bash), and reproducible bioinformatics analysis. My technical background also includes advanced R programming for statistical modeling and bioinformatics, as well as practical Python experience for data processing, automation, and pipeline scripting.

I am currently completing my Ph.D. focused on RNA-seq-based approaches to predict CAR-T cell responses in patients with B-cell lymphoma. My research integrates transcriptomic data analysis, predictive modeling and immunotherapy response stratification, with a strong emphasis on translating high-dimensional sequencing data into clinically relevant predictive insights. The objective of my research is to support decision-making in immunotherapy through robust, data-driven models with translational potential.

This work has been conducted under the supervision of [Dr. Carolina Martínez-Laperche](https://scholar.google.es/citations?user=02SsAfIAAAAJ&hl=es) and [Dr. Ismael Buño](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=zlBlbdYAAAAJ&hl=es), and supported by the Madrid Community International PhD Fellowship. My profile combines computational biology, statistical analysis and applied machine learning in translational oncology, with strong emphasis on reproducibility, containerized workflows (Docker and Singularity) and adherence to best practices in scalable pipeline development.
